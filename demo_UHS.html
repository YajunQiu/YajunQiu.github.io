Project Description

UT Health Services (UHS) is the third most popular website in the University of Texas at Austin with an average daily click of 6000. It serves over 50,000 students in UT campus by provide all kinds of information about UHS medical care and patient education. Information architecture and Interface redesign projects are conducted every two years to meet the needs of students and faculties in UT Austin as well as keep abreast of the latest trends in Web design. The latest UT UHS website redesign project started from October 2015, and will end in May 2016.

Project Goal

The primary goal is to increase the usability of the website both on desktop and mobile devices. Redesign attempts are made based on a survey of over 100 students. As a result, the designer implement flat design and responsive design skills upon over 400 pages, including main page.

My Role & Duties

  • Conduct usability test, survey and collect data for analysis, and propose redesign attempts based on the result of the survey
  • Design and develop over 400 web pages, including index page
  • Conduct research on responsive design principles and frames to find possible solution on UHS redesign project
  • Increase interactivity by JS and JQuery

Tools Used

HTML5, CSS3, JS, JQuery, Bootstrap3, Photoshop
